Create Delicious Frozen Treats at Home

Making ice cream at home is rewarding, fun, and lets you control exactly what goes into your dessert. Whether you have an ice cream maker or prefer no-churn methods, we've got you covered with these simple recipes!

Classic Vanilla Bean Ice Cream

Prep: 20 mins Freeze: 4 hours Serves: 6 Easy

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ¬æ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 vanilla bean, split and scraped (or 1 tablespoon vanilla extract)
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • ¬º teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the cream, milk, half the sugar, vanilla bean seeds and pod. Heat over medium until steaming but not boiling.
  2. Meanwhile, in a bowl, whisk the egg yolks with the remaining sugar and salt until pale yellow.
  3. Slowly pour about 1 cup of the hot cream mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly.
  4. Pour the egg mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining cream.
  5. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon (170¬∞F).
  6. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Discard the vanilla pod.
  7. Cover and refrigerate until completely chilled, at least 4 hours or overnight.
  8. Pour the chilled mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to manufacturer's instructions.
  9. Transfer to a freezer-safe container, cover with parchment paper pressed directly onto the surface, and freeze until firm, about 4 hours.

Chef's Tips

For extra creamy texture, add 2 tablespoons of vodka to the mixture before churning. The alcohol prevents ice crystals from forming!

No-Churn Strawberry Ice Cream

Prep: 15 mins Freeze: 6 hours Serves: 8 Easy

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 cups heavy whipping cream, cold
  • 1 (14 oz) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. In a food processor, pulse the strawberries with the sugar until pureed but still slightly chunky.
  2. In a large bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form.
  3. In another bowl, mix the sweetened condensed milk, vanilla extract, and salt.
  4. Gently fold the whipped cream into the condensed milk mixture until combined.
  5. Swirl in the strawberry puree, creating a marbled effect.
  6. Pour into a 9x5-inch loaf pan or freezer-safe container.
  7. Cover with plastic wrap, pressing it directly onto the surface of the ice cream.
  8. Freeze for at least 6 hours or until firm.

Chef's Tips

For an extra burst of flavor, add 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon juice to the strawberry puree.

5-Minute Ice Cream in a Bag

Prep: 5 mins Freeze: None Serves: 1 Easy

Ingredients

  • ¬Ω cup half-and-half or whole milk
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • ¬º teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 cups ice
  • ‚Öì cup rock salt or kosher salt
  • Optional mix-ins: mini chocolate chips, sprinkles, fruit

Instructions

  1. Place the half-and-half, sugar, and vanilla in a small, sealable plastic bag. Seal tightly, removing as much air as possible.
  2. Place the ice and salt in a larger, gallon-sized sealable plastic bag.
  3. Put the smaller bag inside the larger bag and seal the larger bag.
  4. Shake and massage the bags for about 5 minutes until the mixture in the small bag thickens into ice cream.
  5. Remove the small bag, rinse it briefly under cold water to remove any salt on the outside.
  6. Open the small bag, add any mix-ins, and enjoy immediately!

Chef's Tips

Wear gloves or wrap the bag in a towel while shaking, as the ice-salt mixture gets very cold!
